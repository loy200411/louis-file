LOUIS JACQUET

Explore Artworks

Louis Jacquet was born in Paris in 2004. After visiting museums in Paris, in 2022 he began his artistic training at the Brera Academy of Fine Arts in Milan. In 2024, he continued his studies at the Facultad de Bellas Artes in Barcelona, where he spent a year as an exchange student. There, he concentrated on sculpture and painting, specializing in bronze casting.

His career has been nourished by an assiduous frequentation of museums and archaeological sites. His many trips to Italy - Rome, Florence and Milan in particular - and Greece have brought him into direct contact with Antiquity and the masterpieces of art history. In the manner of a modern Grand Tour, this confrontation with the remains of Greco-Roman civilization was a founding foundation for him.

Louis Jacquet's work develops from a practice in which drawing, painting and sculpture interact with 3D modeling, digital composition, three-dimensional printing and augmented reality. His aim is not to reproduce tradition, but to update it, reinterpret it and inscribe it in new forms capable of opening up a field of reflection that is both aesthetic and symbolic.

Louis Jacquet's artistic research focuses on biblical themes such as the fall of the angels, the figures of martyrs and the Last Judgment, renewed by a contemporary reading, between memory and metamorphosis.

Around this thematic orientation is articulated a particularly dense and structured artistic production. Since 2017, Louis Jacquet has produced around 2,500 drawings, methodically grouped together in 125 notebooks that constitute a source of graphic reflections from which he draws to create his paintings and sculptures.

Louis Jacquet's practice is defined by the search for a balance between the classical and the contemporary. By reinterpreting European traditions using today's technologies, he creates a body of work that combines historical continuity, experimental rigor and an eye to the future.
